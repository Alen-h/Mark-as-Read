Privacy Policy

Mark as Read Chrome Extension

Quick Summary: The Mark as Read extension helps you track which web pages you've read by storing basic page information locally and in Chrome's sync storage. We do not collect personal information or share your data with third parties.

1. Information We Collect

The Mark as Read extension collects and stores the following information when you mark a page as read:

  • Page URLs: The web addresses of pages you mark as read (normalized to remove query parameters and fragments)
  • Page Titles: The titles of the web pages you mark
  • Domain Names: The domain names of the websites you visit
  • Timestamps: The date and time when you marked each page as read
  • Original URLs: The complete original URLs for reference purposes

2. How We Use Your Information

The information collected is used solely for the following purposes:

  • Display visual indicators on pages you've marked as read
  • Show your reading history and statistics
  • Synchronize your marked pages across your Chrome browsers on different devices
  • Provide sorting and filtering options in your reading history

3. Data Storage and Security

Local Storage

All your data is stored using Chrome's built-in storage system:

  • Chrome Sync Storage: Your marked pages are stored in Chrome's sync storage, which allows synchronization across your devices where you're signed into Chrome
  • Local Processing: All data processing happens locally on your device

Security Measures

  • No data is transmitted to external servers
  • Data is encrypted as part of Chrome's sync mechanism
  • Only you have access to your reading history data

4. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not share, sell, or transmit your data to any third parties.

  • No analytics services are used
  • No advertising networks have access to your data
  • No external APIs receive your browsing information
  • Your data remains entirely within Chrome's ecosystem

5. Permissions Explanation

The extension requests the following permissions for legitimate functionality:

  • Storage: To save your marked pages and reading history
  • Active Tab: To read the current page's URL and title when you mark it as read
  • Tabs: To send messages between different parts of the extension
  • Host Permissions (http://*/* and https://*/*): To inject the visual "read" indicator on web pages

6. Data Retention and Deletion

  • Retention: Your reading history is stored indefinitely until you manually delete it
  • Manual Deletion: You can remove individual pages or clear all data through the extension's interface
  • Uninstall: All data is automatically removed when you uninstall the extension
  • Account Deletion: Data is tied to your Chrome sync account and will be removed if you delete your Google account

7. Your Rights and Controls

You have full control over your data:

  • View all your marked pages in the reading history
  • Remove individual pages from your reading history
  • Disable Chrome sync to keep data local only
  • Uninstall the extension to remove all stored data

8. Children's Privacy

This extension does not specifically target children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. The extension functions the same way for all users regardless of age.

9. Changes to Privacy Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. When we do:

  • We will update the "Last Updated" date below
  • Significant changes will be communicated through the extension's update notes
  • Continued use of the extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the new policy

10. International Users

This extension can be used worldwide. All data processing occurs locally on your device or within Google's Chrome sync infrastructure, which complies with applicable international data protection laws.
